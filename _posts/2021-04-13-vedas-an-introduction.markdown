There are many ancient religious text in Hinduism. Vedas are known to be the oldest one. These are the shruti (*that which is heard*) texts, which means that it was not created in a written manner. Ancient scholars used to chant these shlokas and those were passed on to next generations in verbal form. Over the period of time, people got familiar with writing and the disciples of these scholars (read yogi and saadhus) started writing down these shlokas in a form of collection. These are now known as Vedas.

## Parts of Vedas
- **Karma Kanda** (concerned with the ceremonial rites and rituals)
    - Samhitas
    - Brahmanas
- **Gyan Kanda** (focus on the philosophy and spiritualism)
    - Aranyakas
    - Upanishads

# Hindu School of Thoughts
Hinduism has 6 schools of philosophy called Darshanas.
1. Nyaya,
2. Vaiseshika,
3. Samkhya,
4. Yoga,
5. Purva Mimamsa,
6. Uttara Mimamsa.

**Uttara Mimamsa**, also called **Vedanta** has 6 sub-schools
1. Advaita,
2. Vishishtadvaita,
3. Dvaita,
4. Dvaitadvaita,
5. Suddhadvaita,
6. AchintyaBhedabheda.


**Poorv Mimamsa** is generally just called **Mimamsa**. They are the same.<br>
**Uttara Mimamsa** is called **Vedanta**.<br><br>
**Mimamsa** gives the meaning of the Vedic verses. It believes that Vedas are like laws. Repeating the Vedas correctly and with the proper time and ritual will produce results. They don’t believe in god. Veda is its god so to speak. It is all about doing this or that ritual and prosperity here and hereafter. It does not talk about liberation. Only about dharma which is given in the Vedas.

**Uttara Mimamsa** focuses on liberation. It believes in god and that god gives the results of rituals, not Vedas.<br><br>
*Advaita, Vishishtadvaita, and Dvaita are popular sub-schools of Uttara Mimamsa.*

**Dvaita** means duality. Man and god are different. *Liberation means man going to heaven and serving god.*

**Vishishtadvaita** means qualified monism. Man and god are the same, but man is a part of god. Like how a drop of water is part of the ocean. *Liberation means merging in god, serving god, etc.*

**Advaita** means Non-duality. God and man are the same. God alone exists. Everything else is an illusion. *Liberation means overcoming this illusion. That means a man becomes god so to speak. There is only oneness.*
