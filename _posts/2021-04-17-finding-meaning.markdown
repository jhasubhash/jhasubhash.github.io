There are a few questions that are hard to answer. I mean you can answer them but then that would be a very subjective which could be subjected to multiple meanings based on the interpretation of the listener.

### Point of a mortal life

**_What is the point of accumulating wealth, making friends, sacrificing for loved ones, when in the end we are anyway going to lose them when we die?_**<br>
Investing in the future isn’t worth it when it starts degrading your present.

**_Why poor don’t end their lives when the misery far outweigh the pleasure in their lives?_**<br>
Before deciding whether their misery far outweigh their pleasure, we need to decide who we should count as poor. Reports suggest that the top 10% holds 82% of the global health while the top 1% alone owns 45%. Do you still consider yourself rich? I mean the definition of poor/rich is very vague. It's a relative term. Those whom you consider poor actually consider themselves richer than the ones who are poorer than them and also feels that their lives worth living when compared to the ones below them.

### Making sense of something that doesn’t make any sense.

**_What is the point of having Religion?_**<br>
I don’t believe in God. At least, I don’t believe it in the way we have defined it up until this point in time. Obviously, people defend this concept of God by connecting it to faith and how faith can help somebody to overcome their bad experiences. My counter is why not develop faith around something realistic and based on our own experiences. Something which can be debated and validated with logic. Why not develop faith in science and try to discover and explain things with logic and practical theories. Humans evolve with time so how is it sensible to believe in stories (devoid of any logic) created by our ancestors and follow them blindly.  
> "Extremism in science leads to discovery and refinement in our understanding of things around us whereas extremism in religion leads to mass killing and forced conversions."

**_What is morality? How can we say with certainty that something is good or bad?_**<br>
It is kind of a measurement tool that we have in place to keep a check on our actions and to some extent our thought process. It can be governed by religion (fear of God), punishment by law, scientific thought process, and a lot of other factors. It is hard to categorize anything as good or bad. Things are always better or worse. There is no absolute when we start categorizing things. If you were a diabetic person then for you, sweets would be bad but if you were a poor person then the same thing would be a good thing.
